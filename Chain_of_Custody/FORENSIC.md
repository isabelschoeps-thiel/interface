# Forensic Evidence Repository – Intellectual Property and Human Rights Case

## Purpose
This repository documents preserved digital evidence related to the unauthorized acquisition, processing, and exploitation of proprietary intellectual work and associated data. The repository serves forensic, legal, and human rights review purposes.

## Scope of Evidence
The documented evidence includes:
- Databases containing intellectual property and cognitive work products
- Identity-linked metadata
- Behavioral and physiological response indicators used for profiling
- Audit logs and preservation records

## Legal and Ethical Context
The evidence is presented in the context of:
- Intellectual property protection
- Data protection and privacy law
- International human rights standards, including human dignity and protection from non-consensual data-driven experimentation

## Evidence Handling
All data has been:
- Identified through forensic methods
- Preserved in read-only mode
- Cryptographically hashed
- Documented within a complete chain of custody

No data has been altered after preservation.

## Access Restrictions
This repository does not provide raw personal data. Access to full datasets is restricted and granted only to authorized judicial, investigative, or human rights bodies upon formal request.

## Reporting Status
The documented evidence is intended for submission to:
- United Nations mechanisms
- International human rights organizations
- Competent judicial and oversight bodies

This repository documents technical and legal findings. It does not make speculative or metaphysical claims. All statements are based on data structures, observed correlations, and documented preservation procedures. kopp
